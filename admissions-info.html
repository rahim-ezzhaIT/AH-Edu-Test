Government-Funded Degrees & Eligibility – Your Guide to “England Finance Student” Support

Some degree programmes in England are available with full or substantial funding from Student Finance England for eligible students. To be qualified in such funding programmes, you must meet certain residency and nationality conditions, and if you’re eligible, you can study part-time (for instance 2 days/week) or flexibly and still receive financial support.

Key Eligibility Criteria for UK Nationals

  • You must ordinarily live in England (or the Channel Islands/Isle of Man) and your home must be England.
  • You must have been lawfully and ordinarily resident in the UK, Islands or Channel Islands for at least 3 consecutive years before the first day of your course.
  • You must be a UK national (or Irish citizen) or have settled/indefinite leave status.

Key Eligibility Criteria for EU Residents / EEA Nationals

  • EU/EEA/Swiss nationals (or family members) may still access funding if they hold settled or pre-settled status under the EU Settlement Scheme and have been resident in the UK/Islands/EEA for at least 3 years prior to the start of the course.
  • Some EU residents may be eligible only for tuition-fee support (not full maintenance support) depending on their residency history.

Student Finance England – Funding & Loan Support for UK University Students (2023–2025)

Learn how to apply for Student Finance England funding, covering tuition and maintenance loans for UK undergraduate and postgraduate students. Find out eligibility, application steps, and deadlines for 2023–2025.

What is England’s Student Finance?

Student Finance England (SFE) is a service funded by the government. The Student Loans Company (SLC) operates it. SFE provides money to students who qualify. These students will enter higher education in the UK. This includes learners who are undergraduates or postgraduates.

Support might include the following which mainly depends on your course type; and, of course eligibility:

  • Tuition Fee Loans: They are paid directly to your university. It is to cover course fees.
  • Maintenance Loans: The funds are paid to you directly. They will help to cover living costs, like rent. It will help for books and food.

Tuition Fee Loans vs. Maintenance Loans

Tuition Fee Loan covers all costs. Yes it does, of your university fees. These are sent straight to your institution, but. A Maintenance Loan helps, with your living costs while you study there is. How much you get depends on your family income. Your location for study factors too, as well. So, do your living arrangements such as; living at home, or somewhere else? Most full-time students studying as undergraduates, can receive these loans. Some learners, studying part-time or as postgraduates, may qualify.

Postgraduate Loans Perhaps you are seeking a Master’s or a Doctorate, that would be great. You may get a Postgraduate Loan. The sum is around £12,471 for 2024–2025 to use for fees. You can use this, too; for study costs. Funds go straight to you. You use them as you like; it could be accommodation or supplies. No separate maintenance loan exists for postgraduate learners.

How to Apply for Student Finance:

Applications take place, online; so simple. The Student Finance England portal is here:

www.gov.uk/student-finance. Some students (such as migrant workers or EU nationals) may need to submit printed forms (e.g., PN1, EU17N) with supporting documentation.

Required documents may include:

  • Passport or government photo ID
  • Proof of UK residency (for Home students)
  • Payslips, contracts, or proof of employment (for migrant worker applicants)
  • Birth or marriage certificates (if applicable)

Application Processing Times

  • Home or Migrant Worker Applications: Take roughly 8 weeks, from the moment of submission which sounds long. Evidence must arrive first; obviously.
  • EU Tuition-Fee Only Applications: Around 4 weeks.
  • Postgraduate Applications: Typically processed within 3 weeks.

Supporting Evidence & Parental Information

Students under 25 or those married/living with a partner may need to provide additional income details through the PFF2 form, verified by their parents or partner. Submitting all required documentation early helps avoid delays — missing bank statements or residency proofs are the most common cause of late payments.

When Will You Receive Funds?

Once your application is approved and your university confirms your enrolment and attendance:

  • Tuition Fee Loan is paid directly to the university.
  • Maintenance Loan is paid to your bank account within 10 working days after approval.

Making Changes or Correcting Errors

If you need to change course details or correct errors after submission, you can:

  • Log into your Student Finance account → “Change your application”.
  • Or submit a Change of Circumstances (CO1) form to Student Finance England.

Processing updates may take up to 6 weeks depending on the type of change.

Additional Grants & Allowances

Depending on your personal situation, you may also qualify for:

  • Disabled Students’ Allowances (DSAs)
  • Parents’ Learning Allowance
  • Adult Dependants’ Grant
  • Childcare Grant

These are non-repayable and awarded based on financial need.

For EU Students

EU residents who arrived after August 2021 are now classed as international students and are no longer eligible for UK government funding.

However:
  • EU students with Settled or Pre-Settled Status under the EU Settlement Scheme, and who arrived before 31 December 2020, may still qualify for Student Finance support.
  • These students can apply for tuition and/or maintenance loans under the same system as UK students.
Important Notes:
  • This information applies to UK-resident students and eligible EU citizens.
  • International students (non-EU) are not eligible for UK government funding, but may apply for university scholarships or private sponsorships.
